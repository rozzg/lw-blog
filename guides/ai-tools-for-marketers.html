Where do I even start?

That's the question most marketers are sitting with right now. Not whether AI is useful — that part is obvious. But where to actually begin without wasting time on tools that don't stick.

This guide cuts through the noise. It's not a full directory of every AI tool on the market. It's a practical starting point — organised by what you actually need to do as a marketer, with a simple framework to help you build from there.

Step 1

The 3-Layer
Framework.

Before you download anything, understand where AI fits in your work. Think in three layers:

1
Layer One
Create
Content, copy, visuals, first drafts. AI does the heavy lifting on output so you can focus on strategy and judgment.
2
Layer Two
Analyse
Data, audience insights, performance patterns. AI processes what you'd miss at human speed — and surfaces what matters.
3
Layer Three
Automate
Sequences, workflows, repetitive tasks. AI runs the machine while you direct it — not the other way around.
Step 2

Tools Worth
Starting With.

These aren't the only tools out there. They're the ones with the lowest learning curve and the highest immediate payoff for marketers.

✍️
Writing & Copy
Claude
Best for strategy, long-form content, email drafts, and nuanced copy. Handles context well across a conversation — great for thinking through problems, not just generating output.
ChatGPT
Strong for quick ideation, brainstorming variations, and social captions. A solid starting point if you're new to working with AI.
Jasper
Built specifically for marketing copy. Good for teams who want templates and brand voice controls baked into their workflow from day one.
🎨
Visuals & Design
Canva AI
If you're already in Canva, start here. Text-to-image, background removal, and AI copy suggestions — all built into a tool you already know.
Adobe Firefly
Best for brand-safe image generation. Trained on licensed content, which matters if intellectual property is a concern for your team.
🔍
Research & Analysis
Perplexity AI
AI-powered search with cited sources. Replaces hours of manual research for market trends, competitive intel, and background reading.
Notion AI
Useful if your team already lives in Notion. Summarise meeting notes, extract action items, and draft briefs — without switching tools.
⚙️
Automation & Workflows
HubSpot AI
If you're already on HubSpot, explore the built-in AI features for email subject lines, content suggestions, and workflow branching — they're underused by most teams.
Zapier + AI
Connect your existing tools and add AI steps into automations — no code required. A fast way to test AI-assisted workflows without rebuilding anything.
Step 3

Your 5-Step
Starter Checklist.

Use this to get from zero to actually using AI in your work within a week.

01
Pick one tool from the writing category
Use it for your next piece of copy. Don't evaluate yet — just use it. Judgment comes after experience.
02
Identify your most repetitive task
Write it down. The single most time-consuming, low-thinking task in your current role. That's your first automation target.
03
Ask Claude or ChatGPT to help with that task
Note what works and what doesn't. The friction points are telling — they show you where you still need to be in the loop.
04
Try the same prompt three different ways
See how output changes when you add more context, change the framing, or specify the audience. This is how you learn to prompt well.
05
Share what you learned with one colleague
Teaching it cements it. Even a quick Slack message — "I tried this and here's what happened" — builds your credibility as someone who's actually doing the work.
The marketers who thrive with AI aren't the ones who know the most tools.
They're the ones who got started, stayed curious, and kept asking better questions.
